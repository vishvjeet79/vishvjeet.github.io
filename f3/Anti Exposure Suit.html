Anti Exposure Suit

1.constructed with waterproof materials

2.inherent buoyancy of at least 70 N

3.covers the whole body except, where the Administration so permits, the feet

4.covering for the hands and head may be provided by separate gloves and a hood, both of which shall be permanently attached to the sui

5.can be unpacked and donned without assistance within 2 min

6.does not sustain burning or continue melting after being totally enveloped in a fire for a period of 2 s

7.is equipped with a pocket for a portable VHF telephone

8.has a lateral field of vision of at least 120

9.climb up and down a vertical ladder of at least 5 m in length

10.jump from a height of not less than 4.5 m into the water with feet firs

11.swim through the water at least 25 m and board a survival craft

12.don a lifejacket without assistance

13.perform all duties associated with abandonment, assist others and operate a rescue boat

14.o turn from a face-down to a face-up position in not more than 5 s

15.e no tendency to turn the wearer face-down in moderate sea condition

16. be fitted with a light

Light characteristics

1.be of white colour

2.be visible in all directions of the upper hemisphere

3.have a luminous intensity of not less than 0.75 cd

4.have a source of energy for a period of at least 8 h

Note-If the light is a flashing light, it shall, in addition:

1.provided with a manually operated switch

2.flash at a rate of not less than 50 flashes and not more than 70 flashes per minute

Thermal Performance Requirements

1.if made of material which has no inherent insulation, be marked with instructions that it must be worn in conjunction with warm clothing

2.the wearer's body core temperature does not fall at a rate of more than 1.5°C per hour, after the first 0.5 h when worn in calm circulating water at a temperature of 5°C
