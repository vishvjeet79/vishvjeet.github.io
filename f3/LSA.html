General requirements for life-saving appliances?

1. constructed with proper workmanship and materials

2.not be damaged in stowage throughout the air temperature range -30°C to +65°C C and, in the case of personal life-saving appliances, unless otherwise specified, remain operational throughout the air temperature range -15°C to +40°C;

3.if they are likely to be immersed in seawater during their use, operate throughout the seawater temperature range -1°C to + 30

4., be rot-proof, corrosion-resistant, and not be unduly affected by seawater, oil or fungal attack;

5.where exposed to sunlight, be resistant to deterioration

6.be of international or vivid reddish orange, or a comparably highly visible colour on all parts where this will assist detection at sea

7.be fitted with retro-reflective material where it will assist in detection and in accordance with the recommendations of the Organization

8.If they are to be used in a seaway, be capable of satisfactory operation in that environment

Lifebuoys

1.an outer diameter of not more than 800 mm

2 an inner diameter of not less than 400 mm

3.be constructed of inherently buoyant material

4.be capable of supporting not less than 14.5 kg of iron in fresh water for a period of 24 h

5.have a mass of not less than 2.5 kg;

6.not sustain burning or continue melting after being totally enveloped in a fire for a period of 2 s

7.be fitted with a grabline not less than 9.5 mm in diameter and not less than 4 times the outside diameter of the body of the buoy in length

8.The grabline shall be secured at four equidistant points around the circumference of the buoy to form four equal loops

9.if it is intended to operate the quick release arrangement provided for the self-activated smoke signals and self-igniting lights, have a mass of not less than 4 kg

Lifebuoy self-igniting lights

1.be such that they cannot be extinguished by wat

2.be of white colour

3.capable of either burning continuously or flashing (discharge flashing) at a rate of not less than 50 flashes and not more than 70 flashes

4.a luminous intensity of not less than 2 cd in all directions of the upper hemisphere

5.d with a source of energy for a period of at least 2 h

Lifebuoy self-activating smoke signals

1.emit smoke of a highly visible colour at a uniform rate

2.period of at least 15 min when floating in calm water

3.not ignite explosively

4.not emit any flame during the entire smoke emission time of the signal

5.not be swamped in a seaway

6.continue to emit smoke when fully submerged in water for a period of at least 10 s

7.provided with a quick-release arrangement

8.a mass of not more than 4 kg

Drop Test of Lifebuoy/Self-igniting lights/Self-activating smoke signals

be constructed to withstand a drop into the water from the height at which it is stowed above the waterline in the lightest seagoing condition or 30 m, whichever is the greater, without impairing either its operating capability or that of its attached components

Buoyant lifelines

1.be non-kinking

2.have a diameter of not less than 8 mm

3.have a breaking strength of not less than 5 kN

Lifejackets

1. not sustain burning or continue melting after being totally enveloped in a fire for a period of 2 s

2.clearly capable of being worn in only one way or inside-out and, if donned incorrectly, it is not injurious to the wearer

3.at least 75% of persons who are completely unfamiliar with the lifejacket can correctly don it within a period of 1 min without assistance, guidance

4.after demonstration, all persons can correctly don it within a period of 1 min without assistance

5.it is comfortable to wear

6.allows the wearer to jump into the water from a height of at least 4.5 m while holding on to the lifejacket

7.allows the wearer to jump into the water from a height of at least 1m with arms held overhead

8.allow the person wearing it to swim a short distance and to board a survival craft

9.buoyancy not reduced by more than 5% after 24 h submersion in fresh water

10.fitted with a whistle firmly secured by a lanyard

11.provided with a releasable buoyant line or other means to secure it to a lifejacket worn by another person in the water

12.provided with a suitable means to allow a rescuer to lift the wearer from the water into a survival craft or rescue boat.
