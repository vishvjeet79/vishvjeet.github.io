Immersion Suit

1.constructed with waterproof materials

2.can be unpacked and donned without assistance within 2 min

3.not sustain burning or continue melting after being totally enveloped in a fire for a period of 2 s

4.cover the whole body with the exception of the face

5.covering for the hands may be provided by separate gloves which shall be permanently attached to the suit

6.a jump from a height of not less than 4.5 m into the water without damaging or dislodging the immersion suit

7.lift the mouth of an exhausted or unconscious person clear of the water by not less than 120 mm

8.allow the wearer to turn from a face-down to a face-up position in not more than 5 s

9.climb up and down a vertical ladder at least 5 m in length

10.perform normal duties associated with abandonment

11.swim a short distance through the water and board a survival craft

12.buoyancy not reduced by more than 5% after 24 h submersion in fresh wate

Additional Requirement for immersion suit to be worn without a lifejacket

1.fitted with a light

Light characteristics

1.be of white colour

2.be visible in all directions of the upper hemisphere

3.have a luminous intensity of not less than 0.75 cd

4.have a source of energy for a period of at least 8 h

Note-If the light is a flashing light, it shall, in addition:

1.provided with a manually operated switch

2.flash at a rate of not less than 50 flashes and not more than 70 flashes per minute

2.provided with a releasable buoyant line or other means to secure it to a suit worn by another person in the water

3.provided with a suitable means to allow a rescuer to lift the wearer from the water into a survival craft or rescue boat

Thermal performance requirements for immersion suits

With no inherent insulation

1.marked with instructions that it must be worn in conjunction with warm clothing

2.the wearer's body core temperature does not fall more than 2°C after a period of 1 h immersion in calm circulating water at a temperature of 5°C,

With Inherent insulation

the wearer's body core temperature does not fall more than 2°C after a period of 6 h immersion in calm circulating water at a temperature of between 0°C and 2°C

NOTE: If an immersion suit is to be worn in conjunction with a lifejacket, the lifejacket shall be worn over the immersion suit. Persons wearing such an immersion suit shall be able to don a lifejacket without assistance. The immersion suit shall be marked to indicate that it must be worn in conjunction with a compatible lifejacket
