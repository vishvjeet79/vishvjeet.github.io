What are the types of IALA buoys?

IALA(International Association of marine aids to navigation and Lighthouse Authorities) sets out the standards for buoys for purpose of navigational safety.

There are two IALA regions:REGION A and REGION B

Region B comprises of Japan, Philippines ,South Korea, and North, South and central Americas

Region A comprises of the remaining areas

Types of IALA marks

  • Cardinal Marks
  • Lateral Marks
  • Special Marks
  • Isolated Danger Mark
  • Safe Water Mark
  • Emergency Wreck Marking Buoy

Cardinal Marks

Cardinal marks are named by the direction to which the deepest water lies

This means that you should pass the buoy on the named side

The colours of the buoys can also be remembered easily: the cones of the topmark indicates the position of the black band

Lateral Marks

Lateral marks are used to indicate the boundaries of a channel and the side to which the preferred channel lies which a mariner should primarily used

Regardless of the region, Port side mark are always a can(cylinder) or the topmarks are a can, and similarily, starboard side buoys are a cone

The diferrence between Region A and Region B marks is solely in the colours. Region B marks have colours exactly reverse of Region A Marks

The lateral marks indicate:

  • Outer limit of the channel
  • The side to which preffered channel lies

Isolated Danger Mark

Isolated danger marks are erected on an obstruction are used to indicate dangers of limited extent which have navigable water all around them

Colour-Black with one or more broad horizontal red bands
Shape-Pillar or spar
Top mark- 2 black spheres one above the other
Light(When Fitted)-White, Fl(2)
Retro reflector code-Blue and red horizontal band

At what distance should you pass and isolated danger mark?

The isolated danger marks are found in coastal region abd in such places the proximity to dangers and traffic is very close. The distance at which the mark shoukd be passed depends on situation and a distance of 5 cables is safe enough.
